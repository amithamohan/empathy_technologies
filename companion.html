HEALTH CARE ASSISTANT

Like all jobs, there are both upsides and downsides to entering this profession. Benefits of becoming a Health Care Assistant include:

  • Developing Close Relationships with Patients
  • Working in a Fast Paced Environment
  • High Demand for Assistants and Job Security



Challenges that characterise this position include:

Potential employers include hospitals, nursing homes, and companies that provide in-home healthcare. However, regardless of the work environment, the following skills are highly valued for all Health Care Assistants:

  • Sensitivity and Empathy
  • Observation Skills
  • Willingness to Perform Personal Care Tasks
  • Ability to Follow Instructions
  • Positivity and Cheerfulnesss



Your Responsibilities

Because patients depend on Health Care Assistants, it is vital that Assistants take their responsibilities seriously. Ten day to day responsibilities of a Health Care Assistant may include:

  1. Taking Blood Samples: For Assistants working in hospitals, taking and delivering blood samples is a regular responsibility.
  2. Mobility Assistance: If a patient has trouble with mobility, Health Care Assistants help them to the toilet and oversee safe transitions from one place to the next.
  3. Replacing Linens: Health Care Assistants are responsible for ensuring the comfort of patients, meaning that linens are replaced by Assistants daily.
  4. Checking Vitals: A large part of a Health Care Assistant’s day consists of monitoring and recording vitals.
